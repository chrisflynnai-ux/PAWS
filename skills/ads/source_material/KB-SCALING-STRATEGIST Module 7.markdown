B) TAXONOMY
• Scaling Philosophy: The Swim Lane Scale.
    ? Consensus: Scaling is not just increasing budget on one campaign; it is about "Graduation" of creatives from Testing -> Scaling -> Retention [SRC: Sam Piliero | 509].
    ? Metric Shift: Move from Platform ROAS (vanity) to ncROAS (New Customer ROAS) and Incremental ROAS [SRC: Sam Piliero | 832].
• Scaling Phases:
    ? Validation (Level 1): <$10k/mo. Simple structure, finding product-market fit.
    ? Growth (Level 2): 10k?30k/mo. "The Messy Middle." Introduction of the Pack System.
    ? Scale (Level 3): 30k?100k+/mo. Cost Caps, Value Rules, Incremental Attribution.
• Budget Mechanics:
    ? Lowest Cost: For spend consistency (Level 1/2).
    ? Cost Caps: For CPA stability at high volume (Level 3) [SRC: Sam Piliero | 521].

C) FRAMEWORKS (8-12)
1. The 3-Level Scaling System
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 756-771]
• Quote: "Level three is the most advanced part... we are introducing cost caps."
• Summary: A progressive account structure that evolves as spend increases.
    ? Level 1 (<$3k/mo): 1 Prospecting CBO (Broad + 2 Interest) + 1 Retention Campaign. Focus on creative finding.
    ? Level 2 (3k?30k/mo): Introduce "The Pack System" (New Ad Sets for new creative batches). Add Retargeting. Focus on "New Customer" spend.
    ? Level 3 ($30k+/mo): Introduce Cost Caps, Value Rules, and Day Parting. Focus on efficiency and incremental lift.
2. The Pack System (Horizontal Scaling)
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 763-764]
• Quote: "Every time you launch new creatives you launch a new pack... creating a hyper competitive environment."
• Summary: Instead of adding ads to existing ad sets (which resets learning), launch a new Ad Set ("Pack") for every new batch of creatives within the Prospecting CBO.
• Steps:
    1. Create 3-5 new creatives.
    2. Launch new Ad Set in Prospecting CBO: "Broad Pack [Date]."
    3. Meta reallocates budget automatically to the best pack.
    4. Old packs naturally fatigue and get less spend; new packs get tested.
3. Budget Cliff Detection & Recovery
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 770-771]
• Quote: "One ad set gets the majority of the spend and then your new adsets don't have any opportunity."
• Summary: Using "Ad Set Spending Limits" to force the algorithm to test new scaling candidates when a "Spend Hog" is blocking growth.
• Steps:
    1. Identify new scaling candidate getting 0 spend.
    2. Set "Ad Set Daily Minimum" to 1x Target CPA (e.g., $50).
    3. Run for max 7 days.
    4. Recovery: If it performs, remove limit (it will scale naturally). If it fails, pause.
4. Post ID Harvesting (Vertical Scaling)
• Source: [SRC: Professor Charley T | 103-107]
• Quote: "You don't turn off good money... simply start pulling the best performing post IDs and introduce it into the control."
• Summary: Moving a winning ad from a testing environment (Flexible/Dynamic) to a "Control" ad set by copying the Post ID to preserve social proof.
• Steps:
    1. Identify winner in Test Campaign.
    2. Copy Post ID.
    3. Paste into "Control/Scale" Ad Set.
    4. Increase Control CBO budget by 20%.
5. Value Rules Geographic Expansion
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 830]
• Quote: "Group together more and more countries in the exact same campaign and downgrade the bid."
• Summary: Scaling internationally without fragmenting the account by using Value Rules to bid lower for lower-value markets within a single campaign.
• Steps:
    1. Group Tier 1 (US/UK) and Tier 2 (EU/AU) in one Ad Set.
    2. Create Value Rule: "If Location is NOT US, Decrease Bid by 20%."
    3. Allows volume from Tier 2 without destroying US blended ROAS.
6. The "Conditional Formatting" Scaling Rule
• Source: [SRC: Chase Chappell | 870-872]
• Quote: "Scale back what's underperforming and scale up what's overperforming."
• Summary: Visualizing performance to make binary budget decisions.
• Steps:
    1. Set custom metric column rules in Ads Manager.
    2. Green (Scale): ROAS > Target (e.g., 2.0). Action: Increase Budget 20%.
    3. Yellow (Sustain): ROAS = Break-even (e.g., 1.5-1.9). Action: Hold.
    4. Red (Kill/Reduce): ROAS < Break-even. Action: Decrease budget or Pause.
7. Cost Cap Implementation (Level 3)
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 519-521]
• Quote: "Spend is variable... allowing cost per acquisition to stay more flat."
• Summary: Switching from "Highest Volume" to "Cost per Result Goal" to maintain efficiency at high scale.
• When to use: Spending >$1k/day or seeing high volatility.
• Mechanism: You set the price (CPA); Meta controls the volume. Spend will drop on bad days and skyrocket on good days.
8. Incremental Attribution Validation
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 832-833]
• Quote: "It's only focused on conversions that happened because of Facebook... not Facebook slipping in at the last second."
• Summary: Checking the "Incremental" attribution column to verify if a scaling ad is actually driving new customers or just retargeting.
• Rule: If "Incremental Purchases" are >70% of "7-day Click Purchases," the ad is scalable. If <50%, it is retargeting fluff.

D) SOPs (5-10)
SOP 1: Weekly Scaling Review (Monday Morning)
• Source: [SRC: Chase Chappell | 253; Sam Piliero | 763]
• Frequency: Weekly.
• Steps:
    1. Check ncROAS: Is New Customer ROAS > Target?
    2. IF YES: Increase Prospecting CBO Budget by 20%.
    3. Check "Packs": Are any old packs fatigued (Freq >3.0, CPA rising)? Pause them.
    4. Launch New: Deploy 1 new "Pack" (Creative Batch) regardless of performance (Rule of 1:10k).
SOP 2: The "Rule of 1:10k" Production Cycle
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 772]
• Frequency: Weekly.
• Rule: "For every $10,000 you spend per month... you need to upload that many ads per week."
    ? $10k Spend = 1 New Ad/Week.
    ? $100k Spend = 10 New Ads/Week.
• Action: Ensure creative pipeline matches spend velocity to prevent "Creative Starvation."
SOP 3: Ad Set Limit Injection (The Kickstart)
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 770]
• Frequency: On launch of new test.
• Steps:
    1. Launch new Ad Set.
    2. Set "Daily Minimum Spend" = 1x CPA Target.
    3. Set calendar reminder for 7 days.
    4. Day 7: Remove limit. If it hasn't scaled naturally by then, it's a loser.
SOP 4: Cost Cap Transition
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 519]
• Frequency: When scaling past $1k/day.
• Steps:
    1. Identify Target CPA (e.g., $50).
    2. Duplicate winning Ad Set.
    3. Change Bid Strategy to "Cost Per Result Goal."
    4. Set Bid = Target CPA + 20% (e.g., $60).
    5. Set Budget to 5-10x Daily Spend (High budget, capped by bid).

E) HEURISTICS (20-35)
Diagnostic (Identifying Problems)
• IF Platform ROAS > 3.0 but MER is declining, THEN you are suffering from "Attribution Hallucination" (Over-crediting retargeting) [SRC: Sam Piliero | 822].
• IF a new "Pack" gets zero spend in CBO, THEN it is not necessarily a failure; the algorithm prefers the existing winner. Use Ad Set Limits to force a test [SRC: Sam Piliero | 770].
• IF Frequency rises >3.5 in Prospecting, THEN you have hit an audience ceiling with that specific creative angle [SRC: Tier 11 | 724].
• IF Spend is high but New Customer % is <40%, THEN your "Broad" targeting has collapsed into a retargeting audience; launch a new account or restrictive exclusions [SRC: Sam Piliero | 512].
Optimization (Improving)
• IF ncROAS > 1.5x, THEN you have permission to scale CBO budget by 20% [SRC: Sam Piliero | 700].
• IF using Cost Caps and spend stops, THEN increase the bid cap by 10-20% to re-enter the auction [SRC: Sam Piliero | 521].
• IF a creative wins in testing, THEN do not turn it off; replicate it into the Scaling Campaign using Post ID [SRC: Professor Charley T | 103].
• IF spending >$10k/mo, THEN ensure you are launching at least 1 new creative concept per week [SRC: Sam Piliero | 190].
Scaling (Growth)
• IF you want to scale internationally, THEN do NOT create separate campaigns for every country; use Value Rules to consolidate [SRC: Sam Piliero | 830].
• IF scaling a winner, THEN add a banner (e.g., "Black Friday") to the existing winner to refresh it without losing learning [SRC: Fraser Cottrell | 884].
• IF you hit a "Budget Cliff" (efficiency drops at higher spend), THEN introduce a second prospecting campaign with a totally different creative angle (e.g., Founder Story vs. User Problem) [SRC: Matt Shiver | 57].
Troubleshooting (Fixing)
• IF scaling leads to "Junk Leads," THEN optimize for a deeper funnel event (e.g., "Booked Call" instead of "Lead") [SRC: Caden Thompson | 296].
• IF one ad hogs 90% of CBO spend, THEN launch a separate "Testing Campaign" to force budget to new concepts [SRC: Ben Heath | 610].
• IF cost caps aren't spending, THEN switch back to "Highest Volume" with a lower budget to rebuild signal [SRC: Sam Piliero | 519].

H) FAILURE MODES (6-10)
1. Name: The Budget Cliff
    ? Symptoms: Profitable at $500/day, CPA spikes 50% at $1,000/day.
    ? Root Cause: Algorithm exhausted the "Easy" audience for the current creative angle.
    ? Recovery: Launch a new "Concept" (not variation) in a new Pack to unlock a new psychographic segment.
    ? Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 705 (Inferred context from Pack system)]
2. Name: Creative Starvation
    ? Symptoms: Performance slowly degrades over weeks; inability to scale.
    ? Root Cause: Failing the "Rule of 1:10k" (Not enough new creatives to support spend).
    ? Recovery: Immediate "Creative Sprint" to batch 5-10 new static concepts using Nano Banana.
    ? Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 190]
3. Name: Attribution Hallucination
    ? Symptoms: Platform ROAS is 4.0, but bank account is empty (MER is 1.5).
    ? Root Cause: Over-crediting Retargeting/View-Through conversions.
    ? Recovery: Switch to "Incremental Attribution" view; cut Retargeting budget.
    ? Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 832]
4. Name: The Spend Hog
    ? Symptoms: One ad gets 90% of budget; new tests get 0%.
    ? Root Cause: CBO predictive modeling favoring history over exploration.
    ? Recovery: Apply "Ad Set Spending Limit" (Min = 1x CPA) for 7 days.
    ? Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 770]

I) GOLDEN RUNS (3-5)
Scenario 1: The "Messy Middle" Breakout ($10k -> $50k)
• Context: Brand stuck at $10k/mo spend.
• Action: Implement Pack System. Launch 1 new Pack per week.
• Result: Spend diversifies across 4 active packs. ncROAS stabilizes.
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 763]
Scenario 2: High-Volume Stability (Cost Caps)
• Context: Spend 3k/day,highlyvolatileCPA(40 one day, $120 next).
• Action: Switch to Cost Caps (Bid = $60 CPA). Set Budget to $10k/day (Liquid).
• Result: Spend fluctuates (2k?8k) but CPA holds steady at $55.
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 521]
Scenario 3: International Scale
• Context: US market saturated ($50 CPM).
• Action: Add UK/AU/CA to Prospecting CBO. Use Value Rules to bid -10% on non-US.
• Result: CPM drops to $35; blended ROAS increases due to cheaper inventory.
• Source: [SRC: Sam Piliero | 830]

J) COMPLIANCE & GUARDRAILS
• Rule of 1:10k: Do not scale spend without scaling creative production. $50k spend = 5 new ads/week minimum [SRC: Sam Piliero | 772].
• Incremental Truth: Never report blended ROAS to clients/stakeholders as "Performance." Use ncROAS or Incremental ROAS [SRC: Sam Piliero | 832].
• Exclusions: Always exclude 180-day purchasers from Prospecting to ensure accurate ncROAS calculation [SRC: Sam Piliero | 761].
